About Me

I am a GIS and Remote Sensing Analyst with a background in field ecology. With experience in both fields, I strive to serve as a liaison between on-site crews and technical staff. My GIS experience includes satellite based remote sensing, land use and land cover classification, creating data collection apps, producing web based maps, geodatabase management, and spatial analysis. I have worked for Universities, State Agencies and NASA. Check out my portfolio to see some of my work in detail. I predominantly use ESRI's ArcGIS Pro and ArcGIS Online, but I am also familiar with QGIS, Google Earth Engine, and R.

Prior to focusing on GIS, I worked on a variety of terrestrial and aquatic ecology projects across the country including: threatened and endangered species conservation, prescribed fire, wildlfire, stream surveys, oyster reef restoration, and sea turtle nesting surveys. I believe these experiences made me a better GIS professional because they provided me with a contextual understanding of how GIS technologies can augment existing field methodologies.

Apalachicola Bay System Initiative

The Apalachicola Bay System Initiative (ABSI) seeks to gain insight into the root causes of decline of the bay's ecosystem and the deterioration of oyster reefs. Ultimately, the ABSI will develop a management and restoration plan for the oyster reefs and the health of the bay.

I was a research assistant with the ABSI where I was responsible for maintaining, managing, and analyzing data. Provided are two samples of my work.

The first is a web map that shows where 5 different agencies are working in Apalachicola Bay to promote collaboration.

The second is an R markdown file showing the creation of a regression model of oyster heights versus weights, which is useful for understanding health and productivity of oyster reefs.

Webmap, Florida, Apalachicola Bay, R, Statistics, Regression
